i'm hanging myself up here,
under the scorching heat of summer,

until i get withered, not a sprinkle of whatever
remained inside i swear.

so you are to grab a bowl of water, or more,
rushing to revive my dead body
until it's filled up inside.

will you?
then i may ask, out of nowhere
what season is it now?

i once heard spring is the right season for one's
death,

then they changed it to rebirth.

for the balmy breezes that once were my friends,
for you,

i hope it's spring now.

you like spring
and you feel like blooming during
this time.

but i did not see you bloom
decently.

i saw you wither,
and made it
who i am, to me and to you.

so summer,
i like it better,

as much as you
whinge about melting down,

somehow i treasure the sun,
as if i'm the ocean

quietly and patiently absorbing its
power

though i'm draining away.

which sun is mine?
in the middle or on the side
among multiples of suns hanging on the pale sky,

or other skies of cherry pink or cotton blue,
that my eyes haven't reached.

how many seasons
will it take me to move one sun to my
room of all favorites?

i'm still hanging up here,
under the sweltering heat of summer,

trying to imitate you

abandoning my words,

how many summers have passed?
until i get withered, not a sprinkle of whatever
remained inside i swear.

so you are to grab a bowl of water, or more,
rushing to revive my dead body
until i'm an ocean...

then i may ask, out of nowhere
what season is it now?

Concept:
A Summer Gasp is a melancholic contemplation of a boy's anthropomorphic sadness about the meaning of its existence. The website is based on the illusion that the sadness creature sees due to the scorching heat of summer, while hanged upside down.
